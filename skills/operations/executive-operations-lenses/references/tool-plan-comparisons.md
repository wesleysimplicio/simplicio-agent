# Tool plan comparison notes

Use these notes when comparing creator, marketing, or growth-tool subscriptions.

## Core rule
Never compare plans only by monthly price until you classify what each tool sells:
- generation capacity
- distribution capacity
- orchestration capacity

## Comparison checklist
1. Price and billing cadence (monthly vs annualized credits)
2. Media/output units published publicly
3. Approximate output volume (e.g. videos/year)
4. Concurrency limits
5. Commercial-use rights / watermarks
6. Distribution reach (accounts/platforms/posts)
7. Missing data on public pricing page

## Session example
- Topview public pricing exposed generation-oriented units such as credits/year, approximate video output, access to video/image/editing models, and concurrency.
- Post Bridge public pricing exposed distribution-oriented units such as connected accounts, unlimited posts, scheduling, and content-studio access.
- Superscale public pricing exposed partial public information for starter tiers, with clearer generation numbers on higher tiers than on the entry tier.

## Interpretation pattern
- `videos/images/credits/models/concurrency` => generation comparison
- `accounts/posts/scheduling/platforms` => distribution comparison
- `research/agent/workflows/team approvals` => orchestration comparison

## Recommended wording
"X appears stronger for generation volume; Y appears stronger for publishing/distribution. These are different bottlenecks, so the best buy depends on which bottleneck is actually limiting revenue right now."
